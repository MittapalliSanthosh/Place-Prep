Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the study of energy and its transformations. It is a fundamental science that has applications in various fields, including engineering, physics, chemistry, and biology.

The Rankine cycle is a thermodynamic cycle that describes the process of converting heat into work. It is used in steam power plants to generate electricity.

Key concepts: Heat, Work, Thermodynamic Systems, and Energy Conversion.

Heat Transfer

Heat transfer is the movement of thermal energy from one object or medium to another. It is a crucial aspect of thermodynamics and has applications in various fields, including engineering, physics, and chemistry.

Heat exchangers are devices used to transfer heat between two fluids. They are widely used in industries such as power generation, chemical processing, and refrigeration.

Key concepts: Heat, Temperature, Thermal Conductivity, Convection, and Conduction.
